Mavis Moo Memorial Bursary Awards
An amazing family movie enjoyed throughout all ages

So I watched this film for the first time at about 1 AM, so my weariness may have contributed to my opinion of it, however, even now, being rejuvenated, and having watched and read analyses of the film, as well as relistening on loop to its soundtrack, I can conclusively say that this is my personal favourite Disney film of the last few decades, and one of my top three animated films in general (with Rango and Fantastic Mr Fox). The animation and setting is excellent, the characters are entertaining, emotional and different, even the side characters who appear only minimally throughout. While the plot may not be as grand as the other epic storylines of past films, given that the entire setting takes place in essentially a relatively small valley, it was resonant with me. Lastly, one must speak of the music, which is without a doubt some of the best music Disney has produced, and this is coming from a person whose favourite genres are jazz and classical. You won't be able to guess how many times I've replayed the 19 seconds of Dolores' verse from 'We Don't Talk About Bruno'. All in all, a superb viewing experience.
